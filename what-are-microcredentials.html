What Are
Microcredentials?

And Why Should English Teachers Care?

By Fajarudin Akbar

A Clear Definition

Microcredentials are short, focused learning programs that let you build and showcase your expertise in a specific skill area. They act as proof of your targeted professional development.

A Real-World Example

OPEN MOOCs

Many educators are familiar with the free, U.S. government-funded online courses for English teachers worldwide. These self-paced programs are a powerful example of microcredentials.

Visit OPEN MOOCs

Why It Matters

  • Targeted Skills: Focus on exactly what you need to learn.
  • Flexible: Learn at your own pace, on your own time.
  • Cost-Effective: Often free or low cost compared to degrees.

The Output

A certificate or digital badge.

Perfect for your CV, professional portfolio, or LinkedIn profile to visibly demonstrate your skills.

View My OPEN Badges

More Than Just a Certificate

The true power of microcredentials lies in the opportunity to reflect on new knowledge, apply it directly in your teaching, and connect your learning to your classroom practice for meaningful impact.
