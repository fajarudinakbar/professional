Finding Your Path

Exploring Microcredential Opportunities for English Teachers

TESOL International Association – Teacher Educator Interest Section Webinar Series

Presenter: Fajarudin Akbar

Objectives

  • Understand what microcredentials are and why they matter.
  • Explore free (OPEN, TeachingEnglish) and paid (NILE, TESOL) options.
  • Learn strategies for evaluating microcredentials.
  • Connect microcredentials to career growth and lifelong learning.
  • Align microcredentialing with TEIS 2025–2026 theme.

What Are Microcredentials?

  • Short, competency-based certifications.
  • Skills-focused, not full degrees.
  • Usually online, flexible, and stackable.
  • Award digital badges or certificates.

Benefits of Microcredentials

  • Flexible and self-paced.
  • Affordable compared to formal study.
  • Focused on specific teaching skills.
  • Stackable and customizable learning pathways.
  • Shareable badges for visibility and recognition.
  • Supports lifelong learning.

Free Options: OPEN

  • Sponsored by U.S. Department of State.
  • Free MOOCs designed by top universities.
  • Topics: TESOL methodology, teaching writing, English for journalism.
  • Certificates of completion available.

Free Options: TeachingEnglish

  • Offered by British Council.
  • Free online courses on practical ELT topics.
  • Tutor support and peer interaction.
  • Flexible study with global community access.

Paid Options: NILE

  • Norwich Institute for Language Education (UK).
  • 8–10 week tutor-led online courses.
  • Small group, interactive learning.
  • Certificates recognized globally (Eaquals/Cambridge aligned).
  • Specializations: refugees, global citizenship, teacher training.

Paid Options: TESOL International Association

  • TESOL International Association on-demand courses.
  • 6-hour modules with digital badges.
  • Topics: High-Leverage Practices, SEL, 6 Principles.
  • Certificate programs available (e.g. TESOL Core Certificate™).
  • Discounts for members.

Evaluating Microcredentials

  • Do the outcomes match your goals?
  • Is the issuing body credible and recognized?
  • Does the badge show real competency (not just attendance)?
  • Consider cost and time commitment.
  • Seek peer reviews and recognition in your context.

Microcredentials & Your Career

  • Lifelong learning through ongoing skill updates.
  • Stackable qualifications toward higher credentials.
  • Enhance CV visibility with digital badges.
  • Recognized by schools/districts for advancement.
  • Valuable for global and intercultural teaching contexts.

Alignment with TEIS Theme

  • Model of innovative teacher education.
  • Flexible, competency-based, and learner-centered.
  • Reflects best practices in PD and global teaching standards.
  • Empowers teachers to take charge of their professional growth.

Key Takeaways

  • Explore both free and paid platforms.
  • Critically evaluate the quality of credentials.
  • Strategically plan microcredentials for your career.
  • Join learning communities for support.
  • Use today’s handout for resources and links.
